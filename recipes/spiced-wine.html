Spiced Wine

Description

Conditum Paradoxum, also known as spiced wine, was a popular beverage to consume during the roman's celebration of Saturnalia. It was valued enough to be included in one of the oldest cookbooks in human history: the Apicius. This cookbook is what is going to be used as a reference in the creation of this ancient drink. The combination of ingredients should result in a gold full bodied color with a sweet honey taste, with a scent of ginger and pepper.

Ingredients

To make spiced wine at home, you will need:

  • 750 ml of white wine
  • 1 date fruit
  • 1 tsp of black pepper
  • 1 cup of honey
  • 1/2 tsp of fennel seeds
  • 2 bay leaves
  • a pinch of saffron

Extra Items

  • Pitcher
  • Saucepan
  • Fine Strainer
  • Coffee Filter

Steps

Follow along to create your own spiced wine:

  1. Place the date fruit, only 75 ml of wine, and the cup of honey into a suacepan
  2. Boil the content of the saucepan, making sure the honey dissolves while avoiding excessive bubbling
  3. Once the mixture is boiling, lower the temp to a simmer while adding the rest of the ingredients
  4. Place a coveirng on the pan, letting the content simmer for around 10 minutes
  5. Use the strainer and coffe filter to strain the mixture into a pitcher
  6. If any pieces of spice remain, pour back into the saucepan and re-strain (you may do this multiple times)
  7. Add the remaining 675 ml of wine to the pitcher
  8. Stir
  9. Place the wine in any container
  10. Place wine in your fridge, and allow it to cool

Voila, spiced wine. Enjoy, but drink responsibly!
